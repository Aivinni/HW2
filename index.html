How to play

The goal of the game is checkmate the enemy king

Checkmate is when a king is in a position where it will be captured on the next move

How the pieces move

The pawn

On its first move of the game, a pawn can move forward 2 square. otherwise, it can only move 1

The pawn is the only piece that cannot move backwards

White pawns move up the board, from rank 2 to rank 8

Black pawns move down the board, from rank 7 to rank 1

If a white pawn reaches rank 8 or a black pawn reaches rank 1

the pawn can promote to any piece, except the king

The pawn is the only piece that captures differently than how it moves

To capture, the pawn HAS to move 1 square horizontaly, in the direction that it is moving

In the image above, the pawns can capture each other

After capturing the enemy pawn, the pawn moves to the square the enemy pawn formerly occupied

*important* Any piece in chess, regardless of value, can capture any other piece provided it is the opposite color

The Bishop

The bishop can move any number of squares diagonally

Interestingly, this means that a bishop will always stay on the same color it starts on

This is why bishops are often reffered to as the light-squared bishop and black-squared bishop, depending on what square they start on

It cannot jump over other pieces, friendly or not

It captures pieces in its path, to capture, it lands on the square the enemy piece is on

The Knight

The knight moves two square horizontally or vertically and then 1 square vertically or horizontally

It can also be defined as moving one square horizontally or vertically and then one square diagonally on the same direction

This type of motion forms what most people describe as an "L" shape

The knight can "jump" over any piece

That means when moving the knight, you can disregard any other piece unless it is on the square the knight will land on

If the knight lands on an enemy piece, the knight will capture that piece

If a friendly piece is on a square your knight wants to land on, you may not

The rook

The rook is like a bishop that moves horizontally and vertically

It can move horizontally or vertically any number of squares

Like the bishop, it cannot jump over other pieces

It captures a piece by landing on it

The queen

The Queen is easily the most powerful piece in chess, except occasionally the knight, and VERY occasionally the bishop or rook

The Queen moves horizontally, vertically or diagonally an infinite number of squares

The Queen is essentially a rook and bishop combined into one piece

The Queen captures just like a rook or bishop and cannot jump over other pieces, friendly or otherwise

The King

The King is the most important piece in chess, but not the most powerful

The King can move one square in any direction

The King can capture any enemy piece if it does not place him in check

Check is when the game is currently in a position where if it was white to move, white can capture black's king or vice versa

In the image above, the white king cannot move to any of the red squares because it would place him check

In this image, the white king is in checkmate because there is nothing white can do to take the white king out of check

Therefore, The white king will be captured in the next move

Black has won the game
